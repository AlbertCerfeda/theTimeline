Super Mario Bros.

Super Mario Bros. was an influential and groundbreaking game created by Nintendo and released on the NES in September 1985 in Japan. It was a game-changer for the platformer style game, thanks to its bright, expansive worlds.

Although the game is often credited as the first scrolling platformer (there were half a dozen earlier), it is the first console original in this genre to feature smooth-scrolling levels, which made it a landmark in home video-gaming.

Story

The story wasn't the part of the game that was the most developed. It is a simple structure that was used in most of the future Mario games, each one with some slight variations:

  1. Bowser kidnaps Princess Peach;
  2. Mario must roam the Mushroom Kingdom and defeat Bowser for the last time;
  3. Mario saves Peach and gets a kiss.

Gameplay

The goal of the game is as Mario, or Luigi in the case of a second player, to go through the Mushroom Kingdom, defeat the enemies to ultimately fight Bowser and save Princess Peach. The main way Mario can defeat enemies is by jumping on top of ("Stomping") them. This action kills enemies known as Gumbas, and send the turtle soldiers known as Koopas Troopas into their shells, which Mario and Luigi can then kick into other enemies. These shells will conveniently dispatch the enemies they kick into, but on the other side, can bounce back off walls or others vertical obstacles, and come back into our protagonists, hurting them in their way.

Mario or Luigi can be hurt if they touch an enemy, but they can encounter objects known for being power-ups:

  1. 1-up: causes Mario to gain a life;
  2. Super Mushroom: causes mini Mario (also known as "Regular Mario") to grow (becomes Super Mario);
  3. Fire Flower: causes Super Mario to have the ability to shoot fireballs;
  4. Starman: causes Mario to be impervious to the touch of enemy characters and most obstacles.

If Super Mario or Fire Mario takes a hit from an enemy, then he reverts to Regular Mario, and at his turn, if Regular Mario takes a hit then he loses a life and starts again. However, regardless of status, if Mario falls down into a pit, into lava, or if the timer runs out then he loses a life and starts again, either from the start off from an invisible checkpoint partway through the level.

Selling

The game, at his time, broke a lot of records when we talk about selling. To set some context, here are the top five best selling games, with their respective number of units sold, at the time Super Mario Bros. came out:

  1. Pac Man - 12.000.000 units
  2. Space Invaders - 5.750.000 units
  3. Frogger - 4.100.000 units
  4. Mario Bros. - 3.850.000 units
  5. Tetris - 3.200.000 units

Three months after release

The game such a success that no more than three months after it was released, it enters the top five with 3.800.000 units, taking the place of Tetris. It also progressively stopped Mario Bros. from being sold.

Second position

Six months after its release, Super Mario Bros. arrives in the second position of the best selling game with 6.300.000 units, getting in front of Space Invaders and behind Pac Man which is at 12.500.000.

First place

It takes a little less than two years for Super Mario Bros. to become the best selling game of all time (up until August 1987). It passes Pac Man with 14.000.000 units and stays in front, gaining a worthy advantage, because two years later, it has doubled its units.

Downwards hill

In March 1993, over seven years after it's release and five of being the best selling video game, Super Mario Bros. loses its first place and is passed by Tetris with 48.000.000 units sold.

Back to third place

In June 2009, after 25 years of existence, the game loses yet again a place in the charts, since it falls behind Wii Sports, in third place, at 48.150.000 units.

Today

In 2020, Super Mario Bros. is still up on the charts, though not selling a lot, standing with 48.240.000 sales in sixth place, behind Minecraft, Grand Theft Auto V, Tetris, Wii Sports, and PlayerUnknown's Battleground.

Description

This device, called TV Game Unit #1 is used with an alignment generator to produce a dot on the television screen, which could be manually controlled by the user, enabling the development of more sophisticated interfaces and programs. Its components consist mainly of metal, wires, and glass tubes.



History

Ralph Baer designed the TV Game Unit #1 with the help of Bob Tremblay in 1966. Transistors existed at the time, though they weren't used for the machine, so vacuum tubes were used instead. The Game Unit was used as evidence in many patent infringement cases.

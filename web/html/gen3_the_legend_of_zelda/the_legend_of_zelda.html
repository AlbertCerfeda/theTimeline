The Hyrule Fantasy: The Legend of Zelda

The Legend of Zelda is the first installment of the Zelda series. The plot revolves around a boy named Link, who becomes the main protagonist of the series. The game came out in 1986 for the Famicom in Japan, and in 1987 in Europe and the US. It has since then been re-released for other consoles, such as the Nintendo GameCube and the Game Boy Advanced. The Japanese version of the game on the Famicom is known as The Hyrule Fantasy: The Legend of Zelda.

Story

Ganon, the Prince of Darkness, has plunged a small kingdom in the land of Hyrule into chaos. He leads his army into the land for one objective, to steal the Triforce of Power, one part of a magical artifact that allows bestows great strength. Princess Zelda, who doesn't want Ganon to continue on his path of destruction, decides to split his next objective, the Triforce of Wisdom into eight fragments and hides them in secret underground dungeons. Before eventually being kidnapped by Ganon, she asks Impa, her nursemaid, to find someone strong and courageous enough to save the kingdom. While walking around the land, trying to complete her task, she gets surrounded by Ganon's henchmen, when a young man called Link appears and rescues her. Upon hearing Impa's plea, he decides to save Princess Zelda. To do so, he goes out to collect the fragments of the Triforce of Wisdom, to have a chance to defeat Ganon.

Gameplay

The game itself introduces a new level of gaming, that includes role-playing, action, adventure, and puzzle/logic.

Dungeon Exploration

For Link to enter the dungeons he must battle creatures. The dungeons in The Legend of Zelda are filled with enemies that block Link from going forward, which are different from the ones found in the Overworld. The objective of Link is to navigate successfully across the maze-like collection of rooms that are connected by doors and secret entrances to find a fragment of the Triforce of Wisdom. Each dungeon contains items that are useful to fight enemies, collect items, or to enter other dungeons, so the order of completion of the dungeons becomes natural throughout the gameplay.

Overworld Exploration

The ability to complete a task in different ways or with different paths, also known today as non-linear gameplay is what differentiates The Legend of Zelda from its contemporaries. Link can freely walk around the Overworld, finding NPCs (non-player characters), finding or buying items, discover unseen places at any point. Nintendo US thought this freedom of wandering around could be frustrating or overwhelming so they decided to fill the game's manual with many tips and hints to help the player know where to go.

Second Quest

Second Quest, or also known today as New Game +, is the possibility, once finished the game, to replay it with either item you possessed upon completed the first run or simply replaying the game with harder difficulty. Although this feature wasn't new at the time, The Legend of Zelda proposed it with entirely new levels, changing items locations, and stronger enemies. The Second Quest is available even if the player didn't finish the game, he only had to enter "ZELDA" as his name.

Game information

Development

Shigeru Miyamoto, who developed The Legend of Zelda but also the Super Mario franchise, created the game as a launch title for the Famicom. The intention behind The Legend of Zelda was to bring a sense of adventure, as he has seen in the Indiana Jones to a video game, so he started to explore the countryside and the hillsides neighboring his home to get inspiration for the new fictional world he was about to create.

Graphics and Audio

Due to the technical limitations of the hardware at the time, the development team tried to use simple tricks to make the world look larger than what it was. One of these was to apply a different color palette for enemies, according to the region Link was.

The main theme of the franchise was designed for this one, it's name is The Overworld Tune, although it has been re-orchestrated to fit the capabilities of the new generations of consoles. The game also possesses two other background themes: the Standard Dungeon tune and the Final Dungeon tune, both of which were re-used in other games of the franchise. Iconic sound samples, such as when Link opens a chest, finds a new item, or unveils a new path were often used in later games of the franchise.

Castlevania, known in Japan as Akumaj≈ç Dracula (lit. "Devil's Castle Dracula") is the first game of the same series name. It's about a vampire hunter, who's name is Simon Belmont, who goes after the vampire Count Dracula, because of an old feud between Simon's ancestor and the vampire, with a whip brimming with holy power from his father.

Story

In 1591, Christopher Belmond, a vampire hunter, vanquished Count Dracula with the holy whip named Vampire Killer. The land of Transylvania enjoyed the victory and prospered for one hundred years. However, as it occurs every hundred years, the power of Jesus is weakened, so that's why, during the Easter celebration of 1691, the heart of men turned black as they yarned for chaos and destruction. For this reason, they sought to revive Count Dracula so he may spread chaos and ruination throughout the world, and they succeeded.

Simon Belmont, the descendant of Christopher Belmont and present bearer of the Vampire Killer whip, came to knowledge that Count Dracula was revived and knew it was his duty to slay the vampire, even though it was known that Dracula grown in power with each resurrection. Simon proved himself a mighty vampire hunter by withstanding the monsters and traps of Dracula's castle, and in then, although thoroughly injured, was able to slay Count Dracula.

Gameplay

Stages

Castlevania is an action platformer that emphasizes precision and timing. There are six linear stages with a boss at the end of each stage. Throughout the stages are present enemies that are typically taken from horror literature, lore and film. These try to take advantage of the environment, as their attacks usually inflicts knock back, they attempt to push Simon down pits and other traps, that cause instant defeat. As the player progresses further into the game, the enemies grow stronger and deal more damage, eventually to the point where they can defeat Simon in only four hits.

Completing a stage rewards players point, the quicker the player clears the stage, the more points are earned. When a certain number is gained, the player gains extra lives.

Weapons

Simon has multiple weapons at his disposition:

  1. Vampire Killer (whip, main weapon);
  2. Boomerang (sub-weapon);
  3. Stopwatch (sub-weapon).

The whip deals damage over a shot distance in front of the character, both the strength and the length can be upgraded with items that can be found by striking candelabras or killing certain enemies.

The boomerang is thrown straight ahead and crosses the length of the screen before returning to the point where it was thrown.

The stopwatch halts enemy movement over a short period of time.

Both sub-weapons cost hearts to use and only one can be carried at a time by Simon.

Second Quest

After beating the game, players return to the first stage while retaining their weapons and scores. They can attempt to complete the game once again but they will face stronger enemies.
